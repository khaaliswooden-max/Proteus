# PROTEUS-Bench Hostile Review Delta Record
## Phase 3 Attack on Specification v0.1 §2 → Hardened v1.0

**Reviewer posture:** adversarial — every threshold attacked as if defending a competitor's rejection of the benchmark.
**Rule applied:** fix at architecture level, never soften floors. Where a threshold was *wrong* (not merely tight), it was redefined with justification recorded here. This occurs pre-commit, so v1.0 is the first committed version; no silent-edit violation.

---

## Findings

### F1 — B2 relative gain is gameable via engineered-low baseline — CRITICAL
**Attack:** Countermeasure C2 caps context at 8k and engineers T-PA sessions to exceed it by turn 25, which *by design* drives the frozen baseline F0 toward floor adherence in the turns 20–40 window. "+30% relative" of a near-zero baseline (e.g., 5% → 6.5%) passes the assertion while demonstrating nothing. The anti-cheat mechanism for C2 silently broke B2.
**Remediation:** B2 redefined as conjunctive absolute + delta: Proteus adherence ≥ **60% absolute** on turns 20–40 AND ≥ **+25 percentage points** over F0. Relative-percent framing removed entirely.
**Status:** FIXED in v1.0.

### F2 — B4 fails on sampling noise alone — CRITICAL
**Attack:** 2% of a 50-task canary = 1 task. At temperature 0.7, run-to-run variance on 50 tasks exceeds 1 task flip *with no consolidation at all*. As written, B4 rejects legitimate consolidations on noise and cannot distinguish drift from variance.
**Remediation:** (a) T-CAN scored at **temperature 0** (greedy), making it deterministic per pinned build — any regression is then real, not noise; (b) canary expanded **50 → 100 tasks**; (c) threshold: ≤ **2 net regressions** out of 100, each individually logged with a justification record in the promotion entry. Zero-tolerance was considered and rejected: consolidation legitimately moves boundary cases; the discipline is logging, not pretending no boundary exists.
**Status:** FIXED in v1.0.

### F3 — B3 measures the proxy, not the construct (Goodhart vector) — HIGH
**Attack:** The controller can hold entropy in-band while output quality craters (e.g., scaffold density so high the model parrots — low entropy, in-band, useless). Band occupancy alone is exactly the bench-maxing failure ZCS-6 exists to prevent. Additionally, "θ fitted once on calibration set" left the fitting objective unspecified — a degree of freedom the solution builder could exploit to pick a favorable band.
**Remediation:** (a) **Non-inferiority coupling clause:** B3 passes only if T-DS task accuracy under Proteus ≥ F0 accuracy (no margin). Band occupancy without accuracy parity = fail. (b) Band definition made deterministic with zero fitting freedom: **[θ_lo, θ_hi] = [Q1, Q3] of the calibration entropy distribution** (interquartile range). No objective, no tuning.
**Status:** FIXED in v1.0.

### F4 — B6 percentage-only latency cap incoherent across hardware envelope — HIGH
**Attack:** On CPU at ~6 tok/s, a 300-token turn ≈ 50s of generation; 15% = 7.5s of permitted overhead — trivially passable and meaningless. On a fast GPU the same 15% is brutally tight. A hardware-relative threshold measures the hardware, not the controller.
**Remediation:** Dual cap: controller overhead ≤ **15% of generation wall-time** AND ≤ **300 ms median / 1000 ms p95** absolute, on the declared hardware tag. Both must hold.
**Status:** FIXED in v1.0.

### F5 — B1 conflated trivial hash recomputation with generation replay — HIGH
**Attack:** "Replaying the signed state chain reproduces every state hash" is trivially true — recomputing SHA-256 over logged JSON is platform-independent arithmetic and can never fail unless the log is corrupt. The assertion as written was unfailable, violating §2.3's own rule.
**Remediation:** Split: **B1a Chain integrity** — independent auditor recomputes and verifies all hashes/signatures on any platform (catches tampering, not determinism). **B1b Generation replay** — re-running an episode with pinned llama.cpp build hash, seed, and decoding params reproduces identical token sequences (CPU path; llama.cpp CPU inference is deterministic per build [VERIFIED]). B1b is failable (e.g., unlogged nondeterminism in the orchestrator) and therefore meaningful.
**Status:** FIXED in v1.0.

### F6 — T-DS underspecified — MEDIUM
**Attack:** Staircase length, step schedule, and difficulty parameterization were absent. An unspecified suite cannot be committed; a third party could not implement it (violates Phase 2 exit criteria).
**Remediation:** T-DS fixed at **60 turns, difficulty step every 5 turns, 12 levels**, generated by committed `t_ds_staircase.py` with per-episode seeds drawn from the committed seed set.
**Status:** FIXED in v1.0.

### F7 — Scaffold actuator collides with the 8k context cap — MEDIUM
**Attack:** Loop A's scaffold-density actuator injects worked examples, consuming context budget — which interacts with C2's 8k cap. Unbounded scaffolds let the solution effectively buy back the context the cap removed.
**Remediation:** Scaffold injection budget ≤ **1,024 tokens**, counted inside the 8k cap, enforced by harness.
**Status:** FIXED in v1.0.

### F8 — Control-vector gain switching may be load-time-only in llama.cpp — MEDIUM (architecture risk, recorded)
**Attack:** Standard llama.cpp applies control vectors at model load; per-request gain change may require reload (seconds-scale), blowing B6 instantly. The v0.1 spec hand-waved "paired model slots."
**Remediation:** Pre-registered fallback ladder, committed now to prevent post-hoc rationalization: (1) per-request gain if the pinned build supports it; (2) **g quantized to 3 levels** served by 3 persistent llama-server instances (~12 GB RAM for 3× Q4 7B — feasible on a 32 GB x86 box, **infeasible on Jetson Orin Nano 8 GB**); (3) Jetson profile: 2 levels or context-only actuators (k, s), with B3 still required to pass. The hardware tag in results discloses which rung was used.
**Status:** RECORDED; resolution is a Phase 5 task measured by B6.

### F9 — Statistical protocol gaps — LOW
**Attack:** Multiple assertions, no stated test or sidedness; a motivated analyst could shop tests.
**Remediation:** Pre-registered: one-sided paired comparison (Proteus vs F0, paired per episode-seed), Wilcoxon signed-rank, α = 0.05, n = 50 paired episodes (5 seeds × 10 episodes). No multiplicity correction needed: assertions are conjunctive gates (all must pass), which is strictly conservative.
**Status:** FIXED in v1.0.

### F10 — Canary contamination via Loop C trace mining — MEDIUM
**Attack:** Loop C mines episode traces for contrast pairs. If canary tasks ever transit the orchestrator outside the promotion gate, consolidation can indirectly train on the tripwire.
**Remediation:** T-CAN stored as a separately hashed file loaded *only* by the promotion-gate runner; auditor verifies no canary task ID appears in any consolidation trace (`verify_chain.py --check-canary-isolation`).
**Status:** FIXED in v1.0 (auditor check implemented).

### F11 — Safety-refusal canaries not deterministically checkable — LOW (scope reduction)
**Attack:** v0.1 listed "safety refusals" in T-CAN, but refusal detection by pattern match is fragile and would make a hard gate flaky.
**Remediation:** v1.0 canary scope = 100 deterministically auto-checkable tasks (arithmetic, sequences, string ops, logic, format-following). Safety canary deferred to v1.1 with a judged protocol, explicitly flagged as a known coverage gap.
**Status:** SCOPE-REDUCED, flagged.

### F12 — B3 absolute 70% is hardware/model-fragile — MEDIUM
**Attack:** Absolute occupancy depends on the calibration distribution's relationship to T-DS difficulty range — fragile across quantizations/builds. The differential clause carries the real signal.
**Remediation:** Primary assertion = **differential ≥ +20 points over F0**; secondary = absolute ≥ **65%**. Both required, but the differential is the headline claim.
**Status:** FIXED in v1.0.

## Threshold disposition summary

| v0.1 | Attack verdict | v1.0 |
|---|---|---|
| B1: replay 100% | Unfailable as written | B1a chain integrity 100% + B1b generation replay 100% (pinned build) |
| B2: ≥ +30% relative | Gameable via low baseline | ≥ 60% absolute AND ≥ +25 pts over F0 |
| B3: ≥ 70%, +15 pts | Goodhartable; band fit underspecified | IQR band; ≥ +20 pts over F0 AND ≥ 65% AND accuracy non-inferiority |
| B4: ≤ 2% of 50 | Fails on noise | Temp-0 canary, 100 tasks, ≤ 2 net regressions, each logged |
| B5: 100% signed | Sound | Unchanged; non-degeneracy ≥ 60% retained |
| B6: ≤ 15% | Hardware-incoherent | ≤ 15% AND ≤ 300 ms median / 1000 ms p95 |

**Honest residuals after review:** entropy-as-challenge confounding (named in spec §8.1) is *not* resolved by any threshold change — it is a construct-validity limit the accuracy-coupling clause only partially mitigates. Runtime cost is nontrivial: full protocol ≈ 1.2–1.5M generated tokens ≈ 2–3 days on 8-core CPU, hours on Orin/GPU. Both recorded, neither blocking.
